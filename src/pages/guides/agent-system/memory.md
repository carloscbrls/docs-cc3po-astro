# Context & Memory

How your agent team learns and remembers.

## How Memory Works

### What Agents Remember

| Type | Example | Duration |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Your Business** | Industry, audience, goals | Permanent |
| **Your Preferences** | Tone, style, brand | Permanent |
| **Project History** | What we built, when | Permanent |
| **Decisions Made** | Why we chose X over Y | Permanent |
| **Daily Work** | Tasks completed today | Temporary |
| **Session Context** | What we discussed this session | Temporary |

### Where Memory Lives

| Location | What's Stored |
|----------|---------------|
| **MEMORY.md** | Long-term learnings |
| **Daily Logs** | What happened today |
| **Project Files** | Project-specific context |
| **Knowledge Base** | Best practices, guides |

## What We Track

### Business Context
- Your industry
- Your target audience
- Your goals
- Your competitors
- Your unique value

### Preferences
- Writing tone (professional, friendly, technical?)
- Communication style (brief, detailed?)
- Decision patterns (what you typically choose)
- Brand guidelines
- Voice and messaging

### Projects
- What was built
- When it was built
- Why decisions were made
- What worked
- What didn't work

### Learnings
- Mistakes made (and how to avoid them)
- Successes (and how to repeat them)
- Patterns noticed
- Improvements made

## How to Help Agents Remember

### Tell Us Once
When you share something important:
- "Always use this tone..."
- "Never do X..."
- "My audience is..."

We'll remember it.

### Give Context
Instead of: "Write a blog post"
Say: "Write a blog post for my HVAC business targeting homeowners in Manteca. They're worried about energy bills. Use a friendly, helpful tone."

### Reference Previous Work
"This is like the email we wrote last week, but for [different audience]."

### Correct Us
When we get it wrong:
- "That's not my brand voice"
- "This audience is different"
- "Actually, I prefer [X]"

We learn from corrections.

## Memory Limits

### What We Forget
- Details from months ago (unless documented)
- Temporary context (previous sessions)
- Unrecorded decisions

### What We Keep
- Documented decisions
- Learned preferences
- Project history
- Mistakes and fixes

### How to Keep Information
1. **Write it down** — Tell us to save it
2. **Make it permanent** — Ask us to document it
3. **Reference it** — Remind us when needed

## Accessing Your Context

### What You Can See
- All project files
- All decisions made
- All work completed

### What to Request
- "What did we decide about X?"
- "Show me the project history"
- "What's my brand voice?"

### How to Update
- "Remember this for future..."
- "Actually, my audience changed to..."
- "Add this to my preferences..."

## Context For New Projects

When starting something new, we'll ask:
1. What's the goal?
2. Who's the audience?
3. What's the timeline?
4. Any constraints?
5. What worked before?

## Best Practices

### Do This
- Share your preferences early
- Correct us when we're wrong
- Ask us to document decisions
- Reference past work
- Give context with requests

### Avoid This
- Assuming we remember everything
- Changing preferences without telling us
- Skipping context
- Being vague about goals
