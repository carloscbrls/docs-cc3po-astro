**Your website is HIPAA-compliant. Here's what that means and what you need to know.**

---

## What Is HIPAA?

The **Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)** is a federal law that protects patient health information. If your practice handles patient information through your website, your website must be HIPAA-compliant.

**This includes:**
- Patient names
- Email addresses (when combined with health information)
- Phone numbers (when combined with health information)
- Medical information
- Appointment requests
- Contact form submissions

## What Is PHI (Protected Health Information)?

PHI is any information that can identify a patient AND relates to their health:

**Examples of PHI:**
- Patient name + appointment reason
- Email address + health question
- Phone number + "I have a toothache"
- Medical record number
- Insurance information
- Treatment notes
- Prescription information

**What's NOT PHI:**
- General questions (no patient info)
- Newsletter signups (no health info)
- Business inquiries (no health info)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Sending PHI via Email
**Never send patient health information via Gmail, Outlook, or any standard email.**

✅ **Use your patient portal or secure forms instead.**

### ❌ Using Contact Forms for Medical Questions
**Don't ask patients to share medical details in unsecured forms.**

✅ **Use HIPAA-compliant JotForm forms only.**

### ❌ Sharing Passwords
**Never share passwords between staff members.**

✅ **Each person has their own unique login.**

### ❌ Using Personal Devices
**Don't access patient information on personal phones/computers.**

✅ **Use work devices only for PHI access.**

### ❌ Ignoring Security Alerts
**Don't ignore security warnings or updates.**

✅ **Contact us if you see anything suspicious.**
